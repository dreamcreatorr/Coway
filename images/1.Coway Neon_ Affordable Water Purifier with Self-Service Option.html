OUR STORY





Our Mission

With Our Innovation, We Create a Healthy and Convenient Living Environment

Prioritising our customers, Coway's products and services are meant to provide convenience to our customers as well as to keep their everyday life healthy.


Our Vision

Best Life Solution Company

At Coway, we strive to make the world a better, cleaner and safer place. Our future generations deserve the best in everything – clean water, fresh air and a pristine environment. This is our vision. Our dream. Our pledge.

OUR HISTORY

Advancing wellness since 1989.

Established in 1989, Coway has been dedicated to researching and developing environmental solutions, particularly in personal health and wellbeing. With the world’s best specialists and scientists working hand-in-hand, we strongly believe that we can achieve our mission and make our vision a reality.

"Being a market leader in both Korea & Malaysia, we strive to expand our knowledge and diversify our expertise globally, with the aim to make the world a cleaner and purer place to live in."

Our unique competitive advantage is that we complement our high-tech products with an innovative and modern design. In today’s world, the design of a product plays a major role in decision-making, thus prompted us to take that extra effort to deliver not just practical, but innovative design touches.

Through the years, we have progressively improved our Research & Development process with new findings and consumer behaviours. Simply said, this is our bread & butter, the fuel to keep us moving forward and dare we say, we will never settle.

 

RESEARCH & DEVELOPMENT


Coway’s first R&D centre was established in 1993 and a year later, production of our very own products was carried out. The research and development is primarily focused on delivering customer satisfaction and consumer convenience rather than increasing profits for the company itself.

Equipped with 182 cutting-edge devices and equipment, our R&D centre was designated by TÜV SÜD, one of the world’s leading technical service organisations, to run self-analysis and test RoHS (Restriction of Hazardous Substances) electrical and electronic products.

Adding another feather to the cap, our R&D centre was later certified by WQA (Water Quality Association, USA) as a Recognized Testing Laboratory focused on running efficiency and safety tests.

Given the trust from these established organisations, we strive to instil our knowledge and expertise into the products and services especially for our customers all over the world.

Manufacturing Plant

Yugu Factory

After a year of completion, the Yugu factory in Korea expanded and became the world’s largest consumer electronics manufacturing site in 1995. Soon after, the factory started manufacturing filters in 1997 with its high-tech Programmable Logic Controller-based automated facility that produces 1.44million water filtration devices annually.

"Coway is the largest indoor filter manufacturer not only in Korea, but the world."

With a building area of 1,2859.98sqm and a floor area of 20,450.79sqm, the factory is equipped with nine water filtration device lines, four air purifier lines, nine filter lines and a carbon block line with an annual production of over 20million filters, water filtration devices and air purifiers. (as of April 2016)

The factory then focused on the environment and consistency with the aim of achieving zero waste, defects and breakdowns with the introduction of a system that achieves zero-fault during production, an Environmental R&D Centre and Water Analysis & Research Centre (WARC). These introductions have significantly improved productivity and quality and at the same time, reduced costs.

"Every single product goes through vigorous testing over 1,000,000 times."

HAPPY WATER PROJECT

 

Our promise to sustainability:

Malaysia is a country blessed in many ways: a burgeoning nation populated by great people and filled with an abundance of resources.

Yet, home to over 150,000 indigenous residents, the heart-breaking reality is that many of our fellow citizens still struggle with their everyday lives as a result of the lack of resources and aid to carry out a normal living. Drought, pollution, and difficulty in accessing clean water sources are daily challenges these communities face - one we often take for granted.

Now with a presence in over 1 million homes across Malaysia, it is both our responsibility and mission to ensure that no one is marginalised from using what is rightfully theirs: clean water.

We aim to create a bridge of opportunities and possibilities for the underprivileged to inherit what truly belongs to them. Ultimately, we strive to enable and empower all to lead a meaningful and pure life through our Happy Water Project.

 

AND FOR THAT, WE ARE GRATEFUL

Following COWAY Run 2018 and 2019, Coway wasted no time to work diligently to provide potable water to the indigenous communities. To date, up to 285 families from 15 villages in Pahang who live off poor water conditions were aided to access clean water through Happy Water Project.

The Happy Water Project efforts were extensive to ensure that a solid foundation in water hygiene and sanitation is instilled among the communities. This was made possible by first understanding the living conditions of the families: analysing electrical supplies, water sources, sanitation facilities etc.

As a staple water source, we learned that the communities rely heavily on water from rivers or wells for consumption, cooking, cleaning, and others to conduct their daily activities.





Hard work was also balanced with light-hearted moments when Coway volunteers were able to learn from the local villagers to create craft work and memorable moments – elevating the Happy Water Project experience for both the Orang Asli and the Coway family.

 

COWAY CSR

Although many Malaysians have access to clean water to carry out their daily necessities, there are still some of our communities face water pollution and water shortage. For the Orang Asli community who live in remote areas, the community must rely on the nearest water source to accommodate their daily water needs. For many underprivileged communities, the water that they drink, cook or clean with is unsanitary and may lead to diseases. .

We believe that clean water should be a basic human right of every Malaysian, regardless of their status and background.

Armed with the determination to help communities in Malaysia, Coway Malaysia gave life to Happy Water Project in 2017, its first sustainable CSR initiative with the commitment to uplift the lives of the underprivileged and Orang Asli communities.

WORLD’S FIRST COWAY EXPERIENCE CENTRE OPENS IN MALAYSIA


An array of Life-Changing solutions all under one roof




KUALA LUMPUR, 8 March 2021 – Coway recently launched the world’s first Coway Experience Centre at Wisma UOA II on Jalan Pinang, right in the heart of Kuala Lumpur. The Coway Experience Centre will take Malaysian consumers on an experiential journey towards changing their lives with Coway’s innovative products and services for clean water and air, quality sleep and washroom hygiene.“The opening of the Coway Experience Centre is part of Coway Malaysia’s 15th Anniversary Celebration! To date, over seven million Malaysians are enjoying Coway’s Life-Changing solutions, ranging from water purifiers, air purifiers, bathroom appliances, outdoor filters and mattresses. The opening of this centre is one of the many ways we show our appreciation to our customers and the public for their endless support throughout the years,” said Kyle Choi, Managing Director of Coway Malaysia during the opening ceremony last Friday, 5 March 2021.

The historical moment was initiated by Mr Kyle Choi, Managing Director of Coway Malaysia and senior management of Coway’s Sales team and Cody team at the opening ceremony.

“As a Life Care company, we are proud to offer Malaysians a glimpse of how Coway’s R&D and technological solutions can help people live within cleaner and healthier homes and work spaces,” said Choi.

Coway welcomes all to head over to the Coway Experience Centre to experience, feel and understand more about Coway’s product and service offerings. The Coway Experience Centre will feature a Living Care section highlighting the brand’s offerings for the bedroom and bathroom. These include Coway’s premium mattresses, plus washroom appliances such as Coway’s bidets and water softeners. Meanwhile, the Water Care and Air Care sections will take on a more futuristic ambiance, where visitors can see and experience the value that Coway’s innovative water and air purifiers bring.

“The exciting hybrid of physical and interactive digital features at the centre will help visitors understand first-hand how Coway’s products and services can enhance the life of every Malaysian. As an educational platform, our centre will showcase useful facts on water and air, plus tips for better sleep towards a healthier you,” Choi added.

Visitors can also learn about the brand’s milestones in Malaysia and Coway Malaysia’s excellent services delivered with care by their Coway Ladies (Codys), Coway Technicians, Homecare Technicians as well as Health Planners. As Coway always emphasises that great products come with excellent services hand in hand.

To enhance visitors’ overall experience, there will be various interactive touchpoints such as quizzes, digital photo gallery, and also a Malaysian map highlighting the villages supported by the ongoing Happy Water Project (HWP) – Coway’s CSR initiative and many more exciting stations for a fun and enjoyable experience.The exhibition space will also showcase Coway Malaysia’s Brand Heritage and journey since Coway reached our shores in 2006, including its many awards and achievements. Coway was established in 1989 in South Korea and has grown into an international brand; also having subsidiaries in the USA, China, Thailand, Vietnam, Indonesia and Malaysia too.The Coway Experience Centre is opened to the public under strict standard operating procedure (SOP) from Mondays to Fridays (10am to 7pm) and on Saturdays (10am to 2pm). It is closed on Sundays and public holidays.
